Section 1

Executive Summary — the analytical framework

The market for weight-loss assistance apps is undergoing the deepest restructuring in its history. Two simultaneous shockwaves are redrawing its boundaries. The first is pharmacological: the mass adoption of GLP-1 drugs — 12.4% of American adults now take them for weight loss, more than double the rate from early 2024 — is shifting the value proposition from "behavioral dieting" to medical-treatment support. The second is technological: AI photo tracking eliminates the historical friction of manual food logging, enabling an app launched in May 2024 (Cal AI, built by two teenagers) to reach 15 million downloads in under two years.

The category is not declining: it is growing in value (+13% in in-app revenues in 2025 across the Health & Fitness category) but stagnating in volume (+0.8% in downloads). A market that is mature in acquisition terms, but monetizing more effectively — and one where value is migrating. It is leaving generic calorie counters (MyFitnessPal ~−6% in revenues, Lose It! flat) toward two poles: medicalized platforms integrating teleconsultation, GLP-1 prescription and now pharmacy (Noom, WeightWatchers), and low-friction AI apps (Cal AI, Simple, MacroFactor). WeightWatchers' swift bankruptcy — Chapter 11 completed in 42 days in mid-2025, erasing 1.15 billion $ in debt — and the acquisition of Cal AI by MyFitnessPal (closed December 2025) are the two landmarks of this shift: the former number-one restructured by the GLP-1 wave, and the number-one by audience forced to buy the AI innovation it had not built.

For a sophisticated buyer or investor, five dimensions structure the evaluation of offerings:

1Medical integration & GLP-1The strategic fault line. From 0 (pure tracker) to 10 (teleconsultation + prescription + integrated pharmacy). This is what separates today's winners from losers.
2Effective monthly priceFrom 2.5 $ (Cal AI) to ~23 $/month (WeightWatchers), excluding medication: a 1-to-10 spread that segments customers — disposable tool vs. health program.
3AI personalizationPhoto recognition, conversational coach, algorithmic target adjustment. The product battleground since 2024–2025 — and the acquirer's key criterion.
4Support modelSelf-service → AI coach → medicalized program: who accompanies the user, and at what structural cost.
5Functional breadthSingle-function → specialist → platform: extent of coverage (nutrition, activity, sleep, community, health).

This framework — not the list of players — is the key to the rest of the report: the following sections position each player along these dimensions.
