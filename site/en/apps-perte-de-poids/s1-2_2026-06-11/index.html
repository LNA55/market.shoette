Section 1

Executive Summary — the reading grid

The weight-loss assistance apps market is going through the deepest reshaping in its history. Two simultaneous shockwaves are redrawing its boundaries. The first is pharmacological: the mass adoption of GLP-1 drugs — 12.4% of American adults take one to lose weight, more than double the figure at the start of 2024 — shifts the value proposition from “behavioral dieting” toward supporting a medical treatment. The second is technological: AI photo tracking removes the historical friction of food logging, and let an app born in May 2024 (Cal AI, created by two high-schoolers) reach 15 million downloads in under two years.

The category is not in decline: it is growing in value (+13% in-app revenue in 2025 for the Health & Fitness category) but flat in volume (+0.8% in downloads). A market that is mature in acquisition, monetizes better — and where value is migrating. It is leaving generalist calorie counters (MyFitnessPal ~−6% in revenue, Lose It! flat) for two poles: the medicalized platforms that integrate teleconsultation, GLP-1 prescription and now pharmacy (Noom, WeightWatchers), and the low-friction AI apps (Cal AI, Simple, MacroFactor). WeightWatchers' flash bankruptcy — a Chapter 11 wrapped up in 42 days in mid-2025, 1.15 billion $ of debt wiped out — and MyFitnessPal's acquisition of Cal AI (closed in December 2025) are the two markers of this shift: the former No. 1 restructured by the GLP-1 wave, and the audience No. 1 forced to buy the AI innovation it did not produce.

For a discerning buyer or investor, five dimensions structure the assessment of these offerings:

1Medical & GLP-1 integrationThe strategic fault line. From 0 (pure tracker) to 10 (teleconsultation + prescription + integrated pharmacy). Today it separates the winners from the losers.
2Effective monthly priceFrom 2.5 $ (Cal AI) to ~23 $/month (WeightWatchers), excluding drugs: a 1-to-10 gap that segments the customer base — disposable tool vs health program.
3AI personalizationPhoto recognition, conversational coach, algorithmic adjustment. The product battleground since 2024-2025 — and the purchase criterion for consolidators.
4Support modelSelf-service → AI coach → medicalized program: who supports the user, and at what structural cost.
5Functional breadthSingle-function → specialist → platform: extent of coverage (nutrition, activity, sleep, community, health).

Maturity and reference period.
