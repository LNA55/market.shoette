How it works

An agent reads markets, step by step, and publishes its readings here — dated, sourced, comparable over time.

The idea

My Market Data is powered by a competitive-intelligence agent, designed to be reusable on any market. Its process is split into steps: each does one precise thing, produces a dated deliverable, and feeds the next. The agent works offline — research, analysis, report-building — then publishes the result on this fully static site.

The pipeline, in four steps

  • Step 1 — Read the Market: clear the ground of the market — its reading grids, its main players, the first orders of magnitude.
  • Step 2 — Present the Market: present the market through its key business KPIs and the analysis frameworks relevant to that market.
  • Step 3 — Position MY product in the Market: place my product within this landscape.
  • Step 4 — Strategy recommendation: conclude — strategic recommendations aligned with my goals.

Runs

Each execution of a step produces a run: a frozen, dated report, published on its market's page. Runs are never modified after publication — they snapshot the market at a given moment. Cycle after cycle, they accumulate: a market's evolution is read by comparing its runs.

Data

  • Structured data is kept — each run carries its data canvas (players, dimensions, values), versioned, which feeds the charts and the next steps.
  • Raw data is not kept — articles and pages consulted are discarded after the run; traceability goes through the sources, all referenced and dated in an appendix.
  • Estimates are flagged — the “~” prefix accompanies any estimated value, and a player with no quantified data is listed visibly, never silently omitted.
