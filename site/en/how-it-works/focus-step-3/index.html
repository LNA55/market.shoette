Focus on Step 3
Position MY product in the Market

The market has been read (Step 1) then analyzed (Step 2). Step 3 flips the view: it places a product of mine among the players of this market, on the positioning chart already built.

What this step is for

The first two steps look at the world: who the players are, how much they weigh, how the market is structured. Step 3 introduces a new project — mine: it evaluates it on each of the dimensions that structure the market, and places it on the interactive chart among its future competitors. You see at a glance who the project is close to, who it diverges from, and which spaces of the market remain empty.

Like the other steps, each execution produces a dated, frozen run: a positioning note, listed on the market's page. If the project pivots or the market moves, you re-run — and the history of positionings stays available.

How to launch it

In Claude Code, enter “launch skill 3” or “position my project”, with the following inputs:

  • The project — a free description, or a link to its source (for example the project's website: the agent reads it to understand the offer).
  • The market — the agent offers the list of markets already studied; you choose with one click. You can also paste the links of the relevant Step 1 and Step 2 runs directly.
  • Optional — any additional information I have about the market; it's cited as such in the note.

Prerequisite: launching Step 3 depends on the existence of a Step 2 run on the same subject (in addition to the Step 1 run, which carries the chart). If the market hasn't been through Step 2 yet, the skill stops and offers to launch it first. If the material provided isn't enough to evaluate the project on the market's dimensions, the agent asks at most 2-3 targeted questions.

The deliverable

1 · The project enters the Step 1 chart

The skill evaluates the project on each dimension of the positioning chart from the latest Step 1 run — same scales, same conventions as the competitors — then adds it to the interactive chart and to the data table that accompanies it. The project carries a distinctive marker ✺ (rays around the circle, name in bold): impossible to confuse with a market player.

The enrichment is strictly additive: the competitors' data is never altered, and the chart's original date stays intact — a dated note “Chart enriched with information about the new project [name], on [date]” is simply added alongside.

Its market share is 0% by definition — it's a new project. The interesting reading is therefore on the other dimensions: price, model, anchoring, personalization… the ones the reader can assign to the chart's visual channels.

2 · A page dedicated to this run

The skill also generates a positioning note — the Step 3 run page — which contains, in order:

  • The project — its name, a few-line description, the link to its source.
  • The positioning — a compact table: each market dimension, the value assigned to the project, and the one-line rationale; then the link to the enriched chart, in the original Step 1 run.
  • The chart analysis — a paragraph with the important information and reading keys: where the project lands, its immediate neighbours, the empty spaces — and the chart configuration recommended to see it well (the project being at 0% on the vertical axis by default).

The project's values come from its source or from me — they are declarative, and the note says so explicitly, unlike the competitors' values which are publicly sourced.

Reading conventions

  • ✺ next to a name: my project — on the chart, in the tooltip and on the detailed card.
  • Project's market share: 0%, always — a new project doesn't have one yet; its growth is noted “—” (not applicable).
  • Project values declarative (its source, or me); competitors' values sourced; ~ = estimate, as everywhere on the site.
  • Runs stay frozen: Step 3 only adds its project to the chart — nothing existing is modified, and the enrichment note is dated.

Essential additional information

A specificity of this step: its deliverable lives in two places. The positioning note (the Step 3 run) is listed in the Step 3 section of the market's page (for example Mobile weight-loss assistance apps), like the runs of the other steps. The enriched chart, however, stays in its original place — in the market's Step 1 run — since that's what's being completed. The note links to the chart, and vice versa.
