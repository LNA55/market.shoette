Focus on Step 1
Read the Market

A market's first reading: what it's for, how to launch it, and how to read the report it produces.

What this step is for

This step clears the ground of the market: it establishes its reading grids, identifies the players that matter and sets the first orders of magnitude — who weighs how much, who's rising, who's declining. It's the foundation of the whole pipeline: the business presentation (Step 2), the positioning (Step 3) and the recommendations (Step 4) all reason from what it has established.

How to launch it

In Claude Code, enter “launch skill 1” or “read the market”, giving a starting point: a sector, a need expressed in natural language, a geographic area, or a combination of the three. If the request is incomplete, the agent infers the most likely market and displays the assumptions it made at the top of the report; it only comes back with a question when the request is too ambiguous to settle. Each launch produces a new dated run, added to the market's history.

The deliverable

The report reads top to bottom, in this order.

The assumptions made

The report opens on the frame: the exact perimeter of the market studied (what's in, what's out), the geographic area, and how the figures are measured. When the initial request was incomplete, the choices made to complete it are shown here, in black and white — you always know what the report is about before reading it.

The executive summary — the reading grid

Half a page that gives the keys to the market: the dimensions that actually structure competition — the ones a demanding buyer or investor would use to compare offers —, the sub-sectors, the codes and the forces at work. It is deliberately not a summary of the players: it's the grid the rest of the report is read with.

Market share & growth

First the context: the market's maturity stage and the reference period chosen to measure growth (with the rationale for that choice). Then the chart: each player positioned by its weight (height) and its momentum (horizontal) — you see at a glance who dominates, who's accelerating, who's losing ground. Players who seem to matter but for which no quantified data was found are listed below the chart: their absence is visible, never silent.

The interactive positioning

The heart of the deliverable: each player is a circle that encodes up to five dimensions at once. The reader isn't passive — they can change the dimensions assigned to each channel, create layers to compare several views, click a player to open its detailed card (key facts, values, sources), and export the chart as PNG or SVG (the export embeds the legend, the date and a reading key). Below it, the table of all the values for direct reading, followed by the “How to read these values” block: units, currencies, conversions applied, meaning of the scales. The detail of the five channels is in Additional information.

The appendices

The report's traceability and accessibility. A1 — Sources: every reference used, with its link and access date — the report's claims can be verified. A2 — Lexicon: the sector's terms and jargon, defined in French and English, so a newcomer to this market can read the report without a dictionary.

Reading conventions

  • ~ before a value: estimate — the exact figure wasn't public, the order of magnitude is argued.
  • —: information not found, and stated as such.
  • A report is frozen at its date: it snapshots the market at a given moment; changes are read by comparing runs.
  • The “last updated” date appears in the report footer.

Essential additional information

The positioning chart encodes up to five dimensions simultaneously, each on a visual channel. By default, the agent assigns the channels by how important each dimension is for this market — the most structuring one first.
